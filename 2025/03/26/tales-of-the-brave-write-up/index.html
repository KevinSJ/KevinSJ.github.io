Tales for the Brave Write-up (HTB Cyber Apocalypse CTF 2025)

Posted on 03-27-2025 Views:

Hi there, thanks for reading this. My name is Sicong Jiang, and I'm a software engineer based in Auckland, New Zealand. I'm part of the One Byte Bandits team on HTB, and this is my first time participating in a CTF. I decided to challenge myself with a hard forensics challenge, and it turned out to be quite interesting. I hope you enjoy this journey of solving the Tales of the Brave challenge together.

Introduction

Let's read through the instructions first:

Tales for the Brave
In Eldoria, a once-innocent website called "Tales for the Brave" has become the focus of unsettling rumors. Some claim it may secretly trap unsuspecting visitors, leading them into a complex phishing scheme. Investigators report signs of encrypted communications and stealthy data collection beneath its friendly exterior. You must uncover the truth and protect Eldoria from a growing threat.
When debugging JavaScript, ensure you use a Firefox-based browser.

There are several things to note here:

  • Once-innocent website: This challenge involves web security.
  • When debugging JavaScript, ensure you use a Firefox-based browser: We should use Firefox.
  • Encrypted communications and stealthy data collection: This suggests hidden or obfuscated behavior.

HTB Ticket

Since this error was on Telegram’s side, I opened a ticket with HTB on Discord.

HTB quickly resolved the issue, but the bot sent an expired invite link.

HTB staff confirmed the link should be expired. Looking for alternative clues, I forwarded message IDs from 6 to 12 and got a zip file with a password. Extracting it yielded a Windows binary: Brave.exe.

Tricking Brave.exe for the Flag

Reversing the binary seemed necessary, but I lacked the tools. Fortunately, Carl (Spymky) from my team stepped in. He loaded it in Ghidra but made little progress. Switching to dynamic analysis, he considered online sandboxes like Any.Run and Joe Sandbox but opted for a local VM with Wireshark.
